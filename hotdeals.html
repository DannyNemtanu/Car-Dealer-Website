  • Mercedes-Benz CLA 180 Sport

    Performance

    The range starts with a 120bhp 1.6 turbo petrol, but the 168bhp 2.1-litre diesel will be the more popular choice.

    Ride & Handling

    CLA Sport models get ‘comfort’ suspension, while AMG Sport versions have an even firmer chassis. The softer set-up is smoother, although the car still shimmies around over poor road surfaces. Sports suspension brings sharper handling, but you suffer a choppier ride.

  • Mercedes-Benz GLE 550E Plug-In Hybrid

    All-new model has been presented, 2016 Mercedes-Benz GLE 550e Plug-In Hybrid. The 2016 Mercedes-Benz GLE does seem like a new model, but in it is actually the renamed SUV known as the ML model. Mercedes has released details of the lineup of the 2016 GLE models, in that lineup is also their first-ever plug-in hybrid SUV, known as the GLE 550e.

    THE ENGINE RANGE & SPECS

    Under the skin of the GLE 550e, there is a combination of the 329-hp V-6 engine and a 85-kW electric motor, all paired up with the company’s 7-speed automatic transmission. Total power output from the two systems is 436 hp and 479 lb-ft of torque. This system combination is quite similar to that used in the plug-in hybrid S-Class sedan, while the C 350e has the electric motor paired up with a 2.0 l 4-cylinder engine.

  • Mercedes Benz E-Class

    Because the E-Class competes with the Cadillac CTS and Audi A5, it needs to be pretty, and the new face of the 2014 E-Class brings it alive. The interior is cleaned up for 2014 as well.

    The new E-Class is more dynamic, improved to the eye from every angle. Mercedes calls it elegant and poised, and it is that, but it's aggressive too. The front end is especially sporty, while character lines on the sides are cleaner. The tweaks keep Mercedes moving from old man's car to muscle car.

    All the E-Class models come standard with the ECO stop/start system, which shuts the engine off when the car stops. The Mercedes system is less intrusive than the BMW system.
